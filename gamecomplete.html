Carne Asada

×

Carne Asada is a damn fine taco. Marinated skirt steak, typically, but sometimes flank. The steak should be seared a bit. Usually served topped with guacamole, cilantro, and onions. Try the carne asada taco at Tacos Rapidos, which is open 24 hours and has several locations. This one is an old Taco Bell and has the worst ambiance around. Get it to go.

Pescado Asada

×

Pescado Asada, grilled fish, is one of the two types of fish tacos originating from Baja Mexico. It's the lesser of the two but is the healthier option. Try it at El Camaron Loco, which is always busy.

Barbacoa

×

Barbacoa. Oh man, so good. Barbacoa means roasted meat, typically beef or lamb. Potentially my favorite. Very messy. Barbacoa is the origin of the food known throughout the world as Barbecue. So delicious, usually served with sliced raw radishes and a sprinkle of Cotija, a salty cheese like Feta. Get it at El Trompito.

De Cabeza

×

Tacos de Cabeza are made from the head of the cow, which is steamed whole until tender. Typically what you get is the cheeks. So good. A good base for hot salsa. Try the cabeza taco at Patzcuaro's. It's right near my house. Stop by and say hi and bring me some.

Tripa

×

Tripa is tripe - the intestines of the cow. Not my favorite, but very popular. They require a lot of careful prep, so get them at a place that specializes. Try them at La Calle.

Al Pastor

×

Al Pastor is my favorite. It's marinated pork that is prepared like gyros, on a rotating vertical spit. This taco was created when Lebanese folks immigrated to Mexico and brought shawarma with them. Served with pineapple. SO GOOD.

Pescado estilo Baja

×

One of my favorites, the Baja style fish taco is glorious. Firm white fish of any kind lightly beer-battered and fried, much like tempura. Served with slaw on top. Must be from a busy place. Try Los Carboncitos, which has a couple locations.

>

Carnitas

×

Carnitas is my favorite. Pork shoulder that is boiled in pork fat. How can you go wrong? Not a spciy dish so I usually add lots of green salsa. Carnitas California is the spot.

Chorizo Asado

×

Chorizo asado is grilled chorizo - a different experience of chorizo than when it's in a burrito. If the chorizo is good, the taco will be as well. Should be spicy. Try it at Taco Mex, which is so busy that they have a separate outdoor kitchen in the parking lot to keep up. All the tacos are 99 cents. WAAAAT?

Camaron Asado

×

Grilled shrimp. Has to be from a busy place so the shrimp is fresh, but when fresh it's tough to beat. Typically with guacamole and pico. Get it at El Camaron Loco, which is crazy good. See what I did there?

>

Tacos Dorados

×

Tacos dorados are what many people call Taquitos. But when they are called they, they are probably crap. Don't order them at anywhere outside a taco place, because they'll be frozen. Good ones are made to order. Sometimes called rolled tacos, they are fried and covered in guacamole. Must be made fresh and good ones are not filled with ground beef, which is not a legitimate taco ingredient. Try them with tinga, which is chicken in chipotle and tomato. Get them at La Mexicana. Also right by my house. Notice anything about my neighborhood?

Lengua

×

It's often said that if a taqueria doesn't have lengua, it's not a real taqueria. Lengua is the tongue of the cow and it's a major hassle to prepare. But well worth it - very tender and flavorful. Head to La Familia for these.

Chicharron

×

Chicharron is so good. It's the fried skin of either pork or chicken. I like the chicken better. It's a great texture - crispy against the soft tortilla. Try them at Tacos y Salsas, which is good for any type of taco and has the most amazing salsa bar.

Sudados

×

Tacos Sudados, "sweaty tacos" are totally unique. Sometimes called tacos al vapor, they are small tacos prepared by stacking lots of them up in a basket, covered with raw onions. You drizzle lots of hot oil into the basket and then cover it with a towel, so the tacos "sweat". The onions cook, the tortilla crisps up, and the meat steams. Try them at Tacos al Vapor, a truck permanently parked on east Colfax.

Pollo Asado

×

Pollo asado is hyper-marinated grilled chicken. A safe choice for the non-adventurous. Hard to mess it up but easy to make it boring and dry. Try them at Rico Pollo, which does nothing but grilled chicken and has multiple locations.

Nopales

×

The only vegetarian taco on this list. Unique to northern Mexico, Nopal is a type of cactus. It takes a lot of prep, so maybe not one to make at home. Delicious. Get it at Tarasco's on Feds.
